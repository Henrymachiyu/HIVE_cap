ProtoTree

The ProtoTree model reasons with prototypes which are typical representations of a feature. For example, pink skinny long legs is a prototype for Flamingos.

The ProtoTree model makes decisions in steps following a tree structure. Each step in the model contains a prototype learned from a previously seen photo. At each step, the model compares a given photo to the step's prototype and rates their similarity with a score between 0 and 1. If the similarity score is above 0.5, the model judges that the prototype is present in the photo and absent otherwise. The model then proceeds to the next step and repeats this process until it reaches the final step.

Below we show an example ProtoTree model. For the given photo, the model produces a similarity score of 0.23 at Step 1. Hence, the model judges that Step 1's prototype is absent in this photo, and moves on to Step 2-a. At Step 2-a, the model produces a similarity score of 0.78 and judges that Step 2-a's prototype is present in this photo. The model predicts that the bird in the given photo is Species 2. Note that the model never visits Step 2-b.
